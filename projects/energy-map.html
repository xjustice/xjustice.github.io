Case Study: Visualizing Global Energy Consumption

Posted Oct 2024 • Tech Stack: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, Leaflet/D3

Project Overview

Energy is the lifeblood of modern society, but the disparity between consumption and sustainable production is often difficult to grasp. The World Energy Map was born from a desire to make complex global energy datasets intuitive and interactive for everyday users and researchers alike.

This project uses large-scale public datasets to visualize consumption trends, renewable energy shifts, and CO2 emissions across all continents.

The Technical Foundation

The biggest challenge in visualizing global data is the clean integration of geographic information with performance-heavy dataset overlays. I chose JavaScript to handle the dynamic rendering of SVG elements on top of a base map, ensuring a 60FPS experience even when scrubbing through chronological data layers.

The map uses a Choropleth (color-shaded) approach to indicate intensity levels. I developed a custom shader-like logic to interpolate colors based on normalized energy values, making it easy to identify regions of high consumption or rapid renewable growth at a glance.

Data Engineering

1. Normalization and Cleaning

Raw energy data is often messy, with varied units like Terawatt-hours (TWh) and Tons of Oil Equivalent (TOE). Every data point was normalized into a common unit to allow for accurate cross-regional comparisons. I engineered a front-end processing pipeline that cleans and caches this data in the browser's localStorage to reduce redundant server requests and improve load times.

2. Temporal Scrubbing

Understanding energy is about understanding *change*. I implemented a time-slider that allows users to travel back as far as 1990. Managing the state of thousands of data points across a timeline was solved using a binary search tree for quick lookups and optimized DOM diffing to update the map colors without reloading the entire page.

UI/UX Design

To keep the data from becoming overwhelming:

  • Minimalist Legend: A clear, dynamic legend that updates as the user filters by energy type (Solar, Coal, Nuclear).
  • Interactive Tooltips: Hovering over any nation provides a snapshot of its energy mix, enabling deeper dives without navigation.
  • Global Search: A fast search bar indexed by ISO country codes allows for quick jumping between regions of interest.

Conclusion

The World Energy Map is more than just a chart; it is a tool for better understanding our global footprint. By leveraging the power of modern web visualization, I've transformed stale spreadsheet data into a living, breathing map of human progress and environmental responsibility.
